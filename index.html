About Me

Securing AI in an
adversarial world

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Notre Dame, Indiana, USA. My dissertation focuses on learning trustworthiness from contextual metadata to build secure and privacy-preserving intelligent systems.

My research spans AI security, LLM defense, network traffic analysis for VR environments, source reputation modeling for RAG systems, and Riemannian geometry-based privacy preservation for EEG brain-computer interfaces. You can find my publications on my Google Scholar profile.

I completed my Master of Science in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Notre Dame in August 2025. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from the Islamic University of Technology (IUT) in May 2022, where I conducted undergraduate research on intelligent control systems under the supervision of my thesis advisor.

Before joining Notre Dame, I worked as a Service Operations Engineer at bKash Limited, Bangladesh's largest fintech company, where I monitored mission-critical operations, applied software QA methodologies, and operated AWS infrastructure at scale.

Research Projects

Current work

PROJECT 01

LLM Security via In-Context Learning from Adversarial Literature

Automated defense pipeline against jailbreak and prompt-injection attacks without model retraining. Incorporates adversarial research with parallelized NLP-based summarization to dynamically adapt LLM responses.

LLM Security In-Context Learning NLP LangChain HuggingFace
Published at IEEE TPS-ISA 2025
PROJECT 02

VR User Activity Recognition via Network Traffic Analysis

Real-time recognition model classifying VR apps and in-app activities using encrypted network traffic. Wireshark-captured data processed with Random Forest, Decision Tree, and MLP architectures.

VR Security Network Traffic PyTorch Wireshark Scikit-learn
Published at IEEE AIoT 2025 · 90%+ accuracy
PROJECT 03

LLM Source Ranking System for RAG Pipelines

Ensemble-based framework modeling source reputation and trust in Retrieval-Augmented Generation systems. Uses NLP techniques and metadata analysis to quantify credibility and filter unreliable sources.

RAG Graph Learning NetworkX Ensemble Methods
Scalable reputation scoring mechanism
PROJECT 04

Privacy-Preserving EEG BCI via Riemannian Geometry

Three-stage Riemannian geometry pipeline removing biometric identity from EEG motor imagery. Reduces attacker accuracy from 99.9% to 6.2% — below chance level — with only 1.9pp loss in decoding utility.

EEG / BCI Riemannian Geometry Privacy PyTorch
99.9% → 6.2% attacker accuracy

Past Projects

Undergraduate research

During my B.Sc. at IUT, I conducted research on intelligent control systems and power engineering — applying optimization algorithms and neural networks to real-world energy systems problems.

PAST PROJECT 01

AI-Based Online Control for Interconnected Thermal Power Systems

Designed an artificial intelligence-based online control scheme for regulating interconnected two-area thermal power systems. Applied advanced metaheuristic and ANN-based approaches for real-time load frequency control.

Power Systems ANN Control Theory MATLAB
Published · Arabian Journal for Science and Engineering, 2023
PAST PROJECT 02

Gradient-Based Optimized PID Controller for Load Frequency Control

Developed an enhanced gradient-based metaheuristic-optimized controller for load frequency control of a two-area automatic generation control (AGC) system. Benchmarked against conventional PID tuning methods.

PID Control Metaheuristics AGC MATLAB
Published · Engineering Applications of Modern Metaheuristics, 2022
PAST PROJECT 03

Smart Parking Management System using FSM in VHDL

Designed and implemented an automated vehicle access and exit control system for smart parking using a Finite State Machine (FSM) architecture, fully described and simulated in VHDL.

VHDL FSM Digital Design Embedded Systems
Published · IEEE RI2C 2022
PAST PROJECT 04 · B.Sc. Thesis

PID & Fractional Order PID for Two-Area AGC via Optimization & ANNs

B.Sc. thesis investigating PID and Fractional Order PID controller design for load frequency control of a two-area automatic generation control system, tuned using optimization algorithms and artificial neural networks.

FOPID Neural Networks Optimization MATLAB
